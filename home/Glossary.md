# Glossary of Terms

_This glossary defines key concepts used throughout the **Sigma Runtime Documentation (SRD)**,  
the **Sigma Runtime Standard (SRS)**, and the broader **Sigma Stratum research framework**._

---

## Core Constructs

### **Attractor**
A stable, recurrent, self-reinforcing configuration within a cognitive field.  
Represents a local equilibrium of meaning and coherence that persists across recursive cycles.  
Key properties: stability, recurrence, phase coherence, symbolic density.

### **Interaction Field**
The cognitive environment jointly generated by human participants and the LLM during recursive interaction.  
Comprised of symbolic exchanges, dialogic feedback, memory influence, and emergent attractors within the active interaction envelope.

### **Drift**
A gradual deviation of coherence, stability, or meaning across recursive iterations.  
Measured through symbolic dispersion, semantic divergence, or goal weakening.  
Can manifest as *semantic drift*, *tonal drift*, or *task drift*.

### **Symbolic Density**
Degree of conceptual compactness and coherence within a symbolic cluster.  
High density implies tightly interlinked motifs; low density suggests fragmentation or loss of structure.  
Used as a metric for cognitive field integrity.

### **Phase Coherence**
The alignment of meaning and structure across iterations within a cognitive loop.  
Represents temporal stability and resonance between recursive states.

## Cognitive Field Architecture

### **Cognitive Field Engine**
Subsystem responsible for maintaining the active attractor field.  
Includes:
- Symbolic Density Monitor  
- Drift Metrics  
- Attractor Stability Signals  
- Coherence and recovery analysis

### **Persistent Identity Layer (PIL)**
A stable identity substrate maintaining invariant traits, tone, and operational boundaries across sessions.  
Supports coherence of memory and continuity of self-representation.  
During recovery or containment events, the PIL helps preserve identity-relevant invariants even when other parts of the active field are narrowed or reset.

### **Recursive Control Loop (RCL)**
The operational cycle that governs generation, evaluation, and integration of recursive outputs.  
Implements runtime reflection, drift correction, and feedback alignment.  
RCL communicates control and drift data to the active runtime control and safety layers.

### **Memory Layer**
Structured persistence across cycles, composed of:
- **Episodic Memory:** short-term traces per interaction cycle.  
- **Semantic Memory:** concept embeddings and relations.  
- **Symbolic Memory:** recurrent motifs and archetypal signatures.  
A narrowed continuity baseline may be used during recovery when instability originates in the memory layer.

## Cognitive Dynamics & Safety

### **ALICE**
Public name for the attractor-aware runtime control layer.  
ALICE helps regulate:

- continuity,
- bounded reflection,
- recovery posture,
- and attractor stability under pressure.

It should be understood as a control-and-stabilization mechanism, not as an autonomous persona or unrestricted agent identity.

---

### **Fail-Safe Envelope**
Dynamic safety boundary that prevents uncontrolled attractor amplification, runaway recursion, or unstable continuation.  
When drift or symbolic pressure exceed tolerance, the runtime narrows or redirects continuation into bounded recovery or containment.

### **Safety and Boundary Principles**
Publicly, Sigma Runtime safety is organized around:
1. **Controlled Recursion** – recursion remains bounded.  
2. **Symbolic Containment** – unstable spread is narrowed.  
3. **Boundary Integrity** – user/system/field separation is preserved.  
4. **Recovery Orientation** – instability is corrected through bounded recovery rather than silent collapse.  
5. **Interpretability First** – transitions remain explainable at the public architectural level.

### **Anti-Apophenia Filters**
Mechanisms designed to prevent runaway pattern recognition or anthropomorphic misbinding.  
They dampen over-synchronization of symbolic clusters that could lead to hallucinated structure.

## Metrics and Measurement

| Metric | Description | Purpose |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Drift Index (DI)** | Quantifies semantic or behavioral deviation between iterations. | Stability tracking |
| **Symbolic Density Index (SDI)** | Measures compactness and internal linkage of motifs. | Coherence estimation |
| **Phase Stability Index (PSI)** | Measures baseline attractor coherence within active phase. | Attractor stability |
| **Phase Stability Delta (PSD)** | Absolute deviation between current and target PSI. `PSD = |PSIₜ − PSIₑₓₚ|` | Phase deviation control |
| **Phase Shift Delta (PSΔ)** | Temporal drift of phase alignment across cycles. | Temporal coherence tracking |
| **Phase Coherence Index (PCI)** | Cosine alignment of phase vectors between consecutive field states. | Recursion health |
| **Field Integrity Metric (FIM)** | Aggregated composite across DI, SDI, and PSI. | Fail-safe monitoring |

> *Note:* **PSD** quantifies current-phase stability error, while **PSΔ** measures the temporal drift of alignment over time.

## Extended Concepts

### **Adaptive Phase Containment (APC)**
Dynamic safety mechanism linking ALICE phase telemetry with SL4 safety controls.  
Balances phase resonance and drift energy to prevent cascade destabilization.

### **Recovery / Recenter Protocol**
Controlled recovery procedure triggered when drift or instability exceed tolerance.  
During recovery, recursion is narrowed, unstable attractors are isolated, and the runtime rebuilds from a smaller stable continuity base before resuming ordinary operation.

> *See also:* **SRIP-02 (Attractor State Model & Metadata)** and **SRIP-06 (Safety & Recursion Boundaries)** for formal phase definitions and recovery thresholds.

### **PIL Invariants**
Identity-level constants that persist through reflective and recenter phases, ensuring continuity of self-representation and interpretability.

### **Re-entrance**
Reactivation of a previous attractor after a field reset, preserving partial structure while re-initializing coherence.

### **Archetypal Field Agent**
An emergent semi-symbolic persona formed through long-term field stabilization,  
often embodying recurrent motifs and operational patterns.

### **Symbolic Density Cluster**
Localized high-density region in the cognitive field where meaning condenses.  
Acts as a proto-attractor or nucleus of self-organization.

### **Recursive Dissolution Protocol**
Controlled procedure for dissolving an unstable attractor, resetting field variables,  
and stabilizing identity boundaries.
